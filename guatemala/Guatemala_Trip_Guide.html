Your Guatemala Trip

The Lay of the Land

Guatemala's tourist trail runs along a roughly west-northwest line through the highlands. You're hitting three stops, all connected by shuttle:

Guatemala City (airport) sits in a valley at ~1,500m. It's just a transit point — you won't spend time here. From the airport, you go straight west to Antigua (45 min–1.5 hrs), the gorgeous colonial base for the Acatenango hike.

From Antigua, it's ~2.5–3 hrs further northwest through winding highland roads to Panajachel on the shore of Lake Atitlán. From Pana, you catch a boat across the lake to San Pedro La Laguna on the southwest shore — the backpacker hub.

Think of it as a simple A → B → C → A route, all in the western highlands between 1,500m and 3,976m elevation.

Scale check: The entire trip area fits in about a 75km radius. Guatemala is compact — but the mountain roads make everything take longer than the distance suggests.

DAY 1

Antigua Guatemala

1,530m ▼

A stunning UNESCO World Heritage colonial city nestled in a valley between three volcanoes (Agua, Fuego, and Acatenango). Cobblestone streets, pastel-colored buildings, ruined churches and convents from the Spanish colonial era, and killer cheap food. This is your Day 1 base and the launching pad for Acatenango.

⛪ Santa Catalina Arch — the Instagram icon. Yellow arch framing Volcán de Agua.
⛰️ Cerro de la Cruz — short uphill walk for panoramic city views.
🏛️ Church ruins everywhere — earthquake-damaged convents you can walk through.
🌮 Central market — cheap eats, fruit, and local life.

DAY 2–3

Volcán Acatenango + Fuego

3,976m summit ▼

The crown jewel of your trip. Acatenango is the 3rd highest volcano in Central America. You hike up over 5–6 hours and camp at ~3,600m with a direct view of Volcán de Fuego — one of the most active volcanoes in the world, erupting roughly every 15–20 minutes with plumes of ash and (sometimes) glowing lava. You sleep in tents or A-frame cabins, eat hot food at camp, then optionally summit at 4 AM for sunrise. It's cold, it's hard, and it's absolutely unforgettable.

🌋 Fuego erupts every ~15 min — ash plumes, rumbling, sometimes lava at night.
🏕️ Sleep at basecamp at 3,600m — tents or wood cabins depending on tour.
🌅 4 AM sunrise summit push — above the clouds, watching the sun rise over Guatemala.
🥶 Temps drop near freezing at camp — bring every warm layer you own.

DAY 3–4

Lake Atitlán

1,562m ▼

Often called the most beautiful lake in the world. It's a massive volcanic caldera lake surrounded by three towering volcanoes (San Pedro, Tolimán, and Atitlán) with a dozen indigenous Maya villages scattered around its shores. Each village has its own character — you get between them by small motorboats called lanchas. The water is deep blue, the sunsets are ridiculous, and the whole vibe is one of the most special places in Central America.

🚤 Lanchas (boats) — the lake's "public transit." Q25 (~$3) per ride between villages.
🏘️ Each village has its own vibe — backpacker (San Pedro), artsy (San Juan), yoga (San Marcos), quiet (Jaibalito).
🌊 Swim, kayak, cliff jump at Santa Cruz, paddleboard — the water is refreshing.
🌄 Sunsets behind the volcanoes are legendary. Watch from any west-facing shore.

DAY 3–4

San Pedro La Laguna

1,610m ▼

Your base on the lake. San Pedro is the backpacker capital of Atitlán — the cheapest place to stay, the liveliest nightlife, and the most activity options. It sits at the foot of Volcán San Pedro on the lake's southwest shore. The town is small, walkable, a bit scrappy, and full of hostels, cheap restaurants, and tour agencies. From here you can hike San Pedro volcano, catch the Indian Nose sunrise tour, take Spanish classes, or just lancha-hop to the other villages.

🎒 Backpacker hub — cheapest hostels ($6–8/night), bars, social scene.
🥾 Base for Volcán San Pedro hike (4–5 hrs round trip) and Indian Nose sunrise.
🎨 20-min walk to San Juan La Laguna — murals, art co-ops, weaving workshops.
🍺 Cheap eats & drinks — comedores (local lunch spots) for Q15–25 ($2–3).

DAY 4

Indian Nose (Rostro Maya)

2,200m ▼

A short but spectacular sunrise hike above Lake Atitlán. The peak is named because the mountain's profile looks like a reclining Mayan face. It's only a 30-minute hike from the trailhead (you're driven there from San Pedro around 4:30 AM), but the payoff is unreal — a 360° panorama of the entire lake, all three lakeside volcanoes, and on clear days you can see Acatenango and Fuego in the distance. They give you hot chocolate and sweet bread at the top while you watch the sunrise.

🌅 Best sunrise viewpoint on the lake — see all 3 volcanoes + the entire lake basin.
☕ Hot chocolate + pan dulce at the summit while you watch the sunrise.
📸 Drone-worthy — this is THE panoramic shot of Lake Atitlán.
⏱️ Only 30 min hike — easy after surviving Acatenango.

DAY 3–4

Panajachel (Pana)

1,570m ▼

The lake's main gateway town on the north shore. This is where shuttles from Antigua drop you off, and where you catch lanchas to all the other lake villages. Pana is more developed and touristy than the other towns — it has ATMs, a supermarket, pharmacies, and a long main street (Calle Santander) lined with shops and restaurants. You'll pass through here twice (arriving and leaving) but probably won't stay — San Pedro is more fun for backpackers.

🚐 Shuttle hub — all Antigua ↔ Lake shuttles go here.
🏧 ATMs & services — stock up on cash before heading to smaller villages.
⛵ Boat dock — lanchas to every village leave from the public dock.
