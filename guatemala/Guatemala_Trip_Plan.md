## Day-by-Day Itinerary

- **Rest of day** — Explore Antigua at a relaxed pace:

  - Grab cheap eats at the Antigua market or street food stalls

**Hostel options:**

### Day 2–3 — Fri May 1 – Sat May 2: Acatenango Volcano Overnight Hike

This is the highlight — an overnight hike up Volcán Acatenango (3,976m), camping at basecamp with front-row views of the active Volcán de Fuego.

- **Day 2 ~5:00–6:00 AM** — Tour company picks you up from Antigua. Drive to trailhead.
- **Morning–Afternoon** — Hike to basecamp (~5–6 hours). Steep switchbacks through farmland, pine forest, and volcanic terrain. The altitude is real — take it slow.
- **Evening** — Settle into camp. Watch Fuego erupt at night (note: lava activity has been reduced since early 2025, but eruptions/ash plumes still happen — check current conditions). Dinner provided.
- **Day 3 ~4:00 AM** — Optional sunrise summit push to Acatenango peak (~1 hour from camp). Incredible sunrise views.
- **~10:00 AM–12:00 PM** — Hike back down. Shuttle returns you to Antigua.
- **Afternoon** — Shower, rest, celebrate surviving. Grab lunch.
- **~3:00–4:00 PM** — Take a shuttle from Antigua to Panajachel, Lake Atitlán (~2.5–3 hrs, $13–25 USD). Book in advance.
- **~6:30–7:00 PM** — Arrive in Panajachel. If staying on the lake's south shore (San Pedro), catch a lancha (boat, ~Q25/$3) — last boats are usually around 6–7 PM, so check times and have a backup plan to stay in Pana if you arrive late.

**Acatenango tour cost:** ~$50–100 USD per person (includes transport from Antigua, guide, tent, sleeping bag, meals). Book through a reputable company in Antigua like OX Expeditions, Wicho & Charlie's, or Tropicana.

**Altitude & acclimatization:** You're going from sea level (NYC) to a basecamp at 3,600m/11,800 ft. Antigua sits at ~1,500m, so your one day there gives you a partial head start — but it's not a full acclimatization. You do NOT need to stay near the trailhead the night before (the tour picks you up in Antigua and drives you). Most backpackers do exactly this schedule and are fine, but expect the hike to feel harder than the distance suggests. Hydrate like crazy starting on the plane, skip heavy drinking on Day 1, take the hike slow, and bring acetazolamide (Diamox) if you want extra insurance — you can buy it OTC at a pharmacy in Antigua.

**What to bring:** Warm layers (it drops near freezing at camp), headlamp, rain jacket, 2–3 liters of water, snacks, good hiking shoes.

### Day 3 Night – Day 4 — Sat night May 2 – Sun May 3: Lake Atitlán

**Where to base yourself:** San Pedro La Laguna — the backpacker hub. Cheapest hostels, best nightlife, and easy access to hikes and boat taxis to other villages.

**Hostel options in San Pedro:**
- Hostel Fe (social, rooftop, cheap dorms ~$6–8/night)
- Mr. Mullet's (party hostel if that's your vibe)

**Day 4 (Sun May 3) — Full day at the lake:**

- **Early morning** — Indian Nose sunrise hike. Tours leave San Pedro around 4:00–4:30 AM. Short 30-min hike to the summit for panoramic views of the lake and all three volcanoes at sunrise. (~$13 USD including transport, guide, and hot chocolate.)
- **Morning** — Back to San Pedro for breakfast. Walk through San Juan La Laguna (20-min walk from San Pedro) — known for art cooperatives, weavings, and murals.
- **Afternoon** — Pick one:
  - **Option A:** Hike Volcán San Pedro (~4–5 hrs round trip, ~$10–15 with a guide, rewarding views from the top)
  - **Option B:** Lancha-hop to other villages — Santa Cruz (swimming/cliff jumping), San Marcos (chill yoga town vibes), Jaibalito (tiny and peaceful)
  - **Option C:** Kayak on the lake, swim, hang at a lakeside bar
- **Evening** — Dinner in San Pedro. Enjoy the sunset over the lake — it's unreal.

## Practical Tips

- **Money:** ATMs in Antigua and Panajachel work fine. Bring your debit card. Small villages around the lake are cash-only (quetzales). 1 USD ≈ 7.7 GTQ.
- **SIM card:** Buy a Tigo or Claro SIM at the airport or in Antigua (~$5–10 for a week of data).
- **Water:** Don't drink tap water. Buy purified water bottles or bring a filter.
- **Safety:** Antigua and the lake towns are very safe for tourists. Use normal precautions (don't flash valuables at night). Chicken buses are fun but can be sketchy after dark — stick to tourist shuttles for longer routes.
- **Language:** Basic Spanish goes a long way. "Cuánto cuesta?" will be your most-used phrase.
- **Pack light:** You have carry-on only! A 40L backpack is plenty. Bring layers for Acatenango, quick-dry clothes, swimsuit, sandals, and hiking shoes.

## Key Bookings to Make Before You Go

1. **Acatenango overnight hike** — Book a few days ahead, especially for a May 1 departure. OX Expeditions or Wicho & Charlie's are well-reviewed.

Aim for the earliest departure possible — ideally no later than 8:00 AM. A private shuttle (~$100–150 split between two) is worth considering here to avoid the Antigua stopover and guarantee timing.
